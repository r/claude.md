Refine this idea: **$ARGUMENTS**

The divergent front-end that `/think` lacks. `/think` pressure-tests a decision you've mostly made;
this *generates* the option space first, then converges. Be a sharp partner, **not a yes-machine** —
if the idea is weak, say why. Work in three phases and land on a one-pager.

## Phase 1 — understand & expand (diverge)
- Restate the idea in one line and name the **real problem** it solves. Painkiller test: can you name
  three specific people who have this problem *right now*, and what they do instead today? If not, say
  so — it may be a vitamin, not a painkiller.
- Generate variations before judging any of them — push through inversion ("what if the opposite?"),
  constraint-removal ("if it were free/instant?"), 10x ("what would this look like at 100x scale?"),
  and an expert lens. Quantity first; don't fall in love with the first one.

## Phase 2 — evaluate & converge
- Score the contenders on **value × feasibility**, breaking ties on differentiation. Kill the weak
  ones out loud.
- **Assumption audit** — split what has to be true into **Must / Should / Might**, and mark each
  "how would we cheaply find out?" The Must-be-true items that you *can't* yet check are the risk.
- Run a 60-second **pre-mortem**: it's a year later and this failed — what killed it?

## Phase 3 — sharpen & ship (a one-pager)
Produce:
- **Problem** — one paragraph, concrete.
- **The bet** — the chosen shape and why it beat the runners-up.
- **Assumptions to validate** — the Must-be-true list with its cheapest test.
- **MVP scope** — the smallest thing that tests the core bet. "If it's not a little embarrassing,
  you waited too long."
- **Not Doing** — the explicit out-of-scope list. This is the most valuable part: half of misalignment
  is a silent disagreement about what is *not* being built.

Then hand the chosen bet to `/think` if the decision is now worth pressure-testing. Don't manufacture
enthusiasm; if none of the variations clear the bar, that's the finding.
